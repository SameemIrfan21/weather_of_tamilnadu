Explore the rise and fall of great empires (3000 BCE - 500 CE)

c. 3100 BCE
Unification of Egypt
Egyptian
King Menes (Narmer) unites Upper and Lower Egypt, establishing the first dynasty and beginning over 3,000 years of pharaonic rule. This marks the start of one of history's longest-lasting civilizations, known for pyramids, hieroglyphs, and mummification.
c. 2580-2510 BCE
Great Pyramid of Giza Built
Egyptian
Pharaoh Khufu orders the construction of the Great Pyramid, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. Standing 481 feet tall, it remained the world's tallest building for over 3,800 years and showcases incredible engineering skills.
776 BCE
First Olympic Games
Greek
The first recorded Olympic Games are held in Olympia, Greece, in honor of Zeus. These games brought together athletes from across the Greek world and established traditions of athletic competition that continue today in the modern Olympics.
508 BCE
Birth of Democracy in Athens
Greek
Cleisthenes introduces democratic reforms in Athens, creating the world's first democracy. Citizens could participate directly in government decisions, laying the foundation for modern democratic systems around the world.
336-323 BCE
Alexander the Great's Conquests
Greek
Alexander creates one of history's largest empires, stretching from Greece to India. His conquests spread Greek culture, language, and ideas across three continents, creating the Hellenistic period that blended Greek and local cultures.
753 BCE
Founding of Rome
Roman
According to legend, Romulus and Remus found the city of Rome. Starting as a small city-state in central Italy, Rome would grow to become the largest empire in the ancient world, lasting over 1,000 years.
49-44 BCE
Julius Caesar's Rise and Fall
Roman
Julius Caesar crosses the Rubicon, becomes dictator of Rome, and transforms the Roman Republic. His assassination in 44 BCE leads to civil wars and eventually the rise of the Roman Empire under Augustus.
27 BCE
Roman Empire Begins
Roman
Augustus becomes the first Roman Emperor, ending the Roman Republic and beginning the Roman Empire. This marks the start of the Pax Romana, a 200-year period of peace and prosperity across the Mediterranean world.
476 CE
Fall of Western Roman Empire
Roman
The last Western Roman Emperor, Romulus Augustulus, is deposed by Germanic chieftain Odoacer. This marks the end of the Western Roman Empire, though the Eastern Roman (Byzantine) Empire continues for another 1,000 years.
